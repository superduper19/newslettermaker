Newsletter Categorization Brief
You are categorizing cannabis/psychedelics news articles into one or more of four newsletters. An article can belong to multiple newsletters. Use y for a clear fit, ym for a possible fit. Leave blank if it doesn't belong.

THC Newsletter — Cannabis Business & Culture (Column I)
Audience: General cannabis-interested readers, consumers, advocates, industry observers.
Covers: Recreational and medical marijuana legalization news, state cannabis laws, social equity, dispensary industry, cultivation, supply chain, cannabis policy, psychedelics culture and policy, drug policy broadly (fentanyl, opioids, war on drugs), cannabis science and health studies, consumer trends, cool finds, historical cannabis content.
Think of it as: The flagship newsletter — broadest scope. If an article is about marijuana in any meaningful way and isn't purely investor/financial or purely hemp-farming, it probably goes here.
Exclude: Local city ordinances about one dispensary opening, small-town drug busts, international busts from non-significant countries, anti-cannabis propaganda, pure press releases from companies.

CBD Newsletter — Hemp & CBD (Column J)
Audience: Hemp industry professionals, CBD product makers and retailers, hemp farmers, CBD consumers.
Covers: Industrial hemp farming and regulation, USDA hemp programs, CBD product regulation (FDA), hemp-derived cannabinoids (Delta-8, Delta-10, THCA, CBG, CBN), hemp supply chain, hemp building materials/food/fiber/textiles, CBD health studies, CBD product launches (only if significant), hemp state laws.
Key distinction from THC: If the source of the CBD/cannabinoid is hemp (not marijuana), it goes here. If a topic covers both (like CBD from marijuana, or a federal bill affecting all cannabis), it may go in both. A CBD topical brand doing ads does NOT go here. USDA approving a state hemp plan? Yes.
Exclude: Ads for CBD brands, generic "CBD helps X condition" pieces with no substance, pure press releases, articles from sites that are obviously CBD product stores.

INV Newsletter — Cannabis Industry & Investment as well as International News (Column K)
Audience: Cannabis investors, B2B operators, MSOs, entrepreneurs, finance/business professionals.
Covers: M&A deals (acquisitions, mergers — with dollar amounts), cannabis stocks and financial summaries, fundraising rounds, major operator news, cannabis banking and finance, international market development (markets poised for growth), licensing/regulatory frameworks affecting the business ecosystem, cannabis real estate/facilities, major business strategy moves by significant operators. Any non usa cannabis news will go here.
Key distinction: This is about money, markets, and business. A state legalizing cannabis goes in THC; the business opportunity that creates goes in INV. A company raising $100M goes in INV. A new executive hire does NOT.
Exclude: Press releases for small companies, new employee hires, company announcements without meaningful financial details, local revenue reports for individual states (e.g., "Ohio made $5M this month"), pure PR pieces.

Med Newsletter — Medical & Health (Column E)
Audience: Healthcare-adjacent readers, medical professionals, patients, public health advocates.
Covers: Opioid/fentanyl crisis (overdose statistics, policy, treatment), clinical trials and study results for cannabis/CBD/psychedelics as medicine, psilocybin/MDMA therapy research, cannabis for specific conditions (PTSD, cancer, pain, anxiety, epilepsy, autism), public health cannabis policy, workplace drug testing reform from a health angle, FDA drug approvals.
Key distinction: The medical angle needs to be substantive — a real study with results, a major policy change affecting patient access, or a meaningful health outcome. "CBD helps sleep" with no study = not Med. A published clinical trial on CBD for focal seizures = yes.
Exclude: Studies that haven't started yet (announce when results come), anti-cannabis health scare pieces, direct study papers (we post articles about studies, not the studies themselves), purely opioid content with no cannabis/psychedelic connection.
Often we will look at the articles about studies from the THC and CBD and INV newsletters and include them. We will also include article about the opioid epidemic. There is overlap with this newsletter and the others. nothing goes here that is not in the other newsletters.

Universal Rejection Rules (do not categorize these at all)

Do not use news roudups though you can gather links from there.
Do not include advertisements or pr releases for conferences though if its something important that was disclosed in a conference its okay.
Advertisements and PR — any article that promotes a specific company's product, links to their store, or is clearly written to benefit one brand
Press releases — company announcements, new hires, expansion announcements without a real editorial source
Paywalled content — if the article requires a subscription to read
Bills that haven't passed — unless the bill is extremely significant and the article explicitly says it has a strong chance of passing; prefer to cover after it passes
Purely local ordinances — one city allowing/banning cannabis delivery, one county issuing a warning, local tax adjustments; nothing that affects fewer than a major metro area
Duplicate topics — if this week already covered the same subject, skip it
International busts — drug seizures from other countries are not newsworthy for this audience
Anti-cannabis propaganda — articles arguing cannabis is dangerous, causes mental illness, or should remain illegal; do not platform these
Old articles — anything more than 2 weeks old unless it's a verified cool find with evergreen value
Too short / no substance — articles under ~300 words or that say nothing beyond what the headline conveys
Opinion/editorial masquerading as news — flag but do not categorize
If its a pharmaceutical solution for opioids we aren't promoting that.


Edge Cases

CBD from marijuana = THC or Med newsletter, NOT CBD (CBD newsletter is hemp-derived only)
Psychedelics = THC newsletter for culture/policy, Med for clinical research, INV for investment
Federal legalization news = THC + INV (big business impact) 
Cannabis tourism = THC only unless it's a major market investment story
